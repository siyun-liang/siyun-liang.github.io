Siyun Liang


I am an M.Sc. student in Informatics at the Technical University of Munich (Munich, Germany), currently completing my master's thesis under the supervision of Dr. Michael Niemeyer and PD. Dr. Federico Tombari. Previously, I interned at the National Institute of Informatics(Tokyo, Japan), where I was supervised by Dr. Huy H. Nguyen and Prof. Dr. Isao Echizen.

During my master studies, I also served as a teaching assistant for the Game Physics course, taught by Prof. Dr. Nils Thuerey, in the Winter Semesters of 2022 and 2023.

Prior to this, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Software Engineering from Southeast University(Nanjing, China), where I completed my bachelor's thesis at SAP Labs China.

My research interests include 3D/4D reconstruction, 3D understanding, and physics simulation. Currently, I am focused on open-vocabulary 3D understanding, leveraging knowledge distillation from 2D foundation models.

I am actively seeking Ph.D. opportunities for the Spring or Fall 2025 intake.
